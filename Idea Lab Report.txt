ABSTRACT 

Partial theft has emerged as a significant yet often unnoticed challenge in modern warehouse 

management, logistics operations, and inventory control systems. Unlike complete theft, which 

involves the removal of entire goods or assets and is easily detectable, partial theft refers to the 

gradual and unauthorized removal of items in small quantities over time. This type of theft is 

particularly difficult to identify using traditional security systems, as it does not trigger standard 

alarms such as forced entry detection or perimeter breach alerts. 

The proposed project, “Partial Theft Detection with Sensors and Alert Modules,” presents 

an advanced IoT-based solution designed to address this gap in security systems. The system 

integrates multiple sensing technologies, including load cells for weight measurement, infrared 

sensors for presence detection, magnetic reed switches for tamper detection, and vibration 

sensors for identifying physical disturbances. These sensors work collaboratively through a 

concept known as multi-sensor fusion, which enhances detection accuracy and minimizes false 

negatives. 

A microcontroller, such as Arduino Uno or NodeMCU, serves as the central processing unit of 

the system. It continuously collects and analyses data from all connected sensors and applies 

predefined threshold logic to detect anomalies. When a suspicious event is identified—such as a 

drop in weight beyond a specified limit or unauthorized opening of a storage container—the 

system immediately triggers alert mechanisms. 

The alert system consists of both local and remote notification methods. Locally, the system uses 

buzzers, LEDs, and LCD displays to provide instant feedback to nearby personnel. Remotely, it 

utilizes GSM modules for SMS alerts and WiFi modules for cloud-based monitoring, enabling 

real-time notifications regardless of location. 

This project demonstrates that by combining low-cost hardware components with IoT 

technology, it is possible to build a highly effective, scalable, and reliable system for detecting 

partial theft. The solution is particularly beneficial for warehouses, retail storage units, and 

supply chain environments, where even minor losses can accumulate into significant financial 

damage over time. 

 

      

  



INTRODUCTION 

In today’s rapidly evolving industrial and commercial environments, ensuring the security of goods 

and assets is of paramount importance. Warehouses, retail stores, and logistics hubs rely heavily on 

inventory management systems to track and safeguard products. However, while significant 

advancements have been made in detecting large-scale theft and unauthorized access, one critical issue 

remains largely unaddressed—partial theft. 

Partial theft refers to the unauthorized removal of a portion of goods rather than the entire stock. This 

type of theft is often carried out gradually, making it difficult to detect through conventional security 

measures. Traditional systems such as CCTV cameras, alarm systems, and access control mechanisms 

are primarily designed to detect visible breaches or complete removal of assets. As a result, small and 

incremental losses often go unnoticed until periodic inventory audits are conducted. 

The inability to detect partial theft in real time leads to several challenges. Organizations may 

experience financial losses, inaccurate inventory records, and operational inefficiencies. In some cases, 

insider threats contribute significantly to such thefts, as employees may exploit the lack of monitoring 

systems for small-scale removals. 

To overcome these challenges, the proposed system introduces a sensor-based intelligent monitoring 

solution. The system employs multiple sensors to track different aspects of the storage environment: 

 Weight monitoring to detect changes in stored goods  

 Presence detection to confirm object availability  

 Tamper detection to identify unauthorized access  

 Vibration sensing to capture physical disturbances  

By combining these sensors, the system creates a multi-layered security approach that significantly 

improves detection accuracy. 

Furthermore, the integration of Internet of Things (IoT) technology enhances the system’s 

capabilities by enabling real-time communication and remote monitoring. Users can receive alerts 

through mobile devices, cloud platforms, or SMS notifications, ensuring immediate awareness of any 

suspicious activity. 

This project represents a shift from traditional reactive security systems to proactive and intelligent 

monitoring solutions, capable of identifying even the smallest irregularities in inventory handling. 

 

 

 

 
 



PROBLEM STATEMENT 

Partial theft is a critical and often overlooked issue in inventory management and 

security systems, especially in large-scale environments such as warehouses, 

distribution centres, and retail storage units. Unlike full-scale theft, which involves 

immediate and noticeable removal of goods, partial theft occurs gradually in small 

quantities and often goes undetected for long periods. This makes it difficult to identify 

and control using traditional security methods. 

One of the major challenges is the gradual removal of items, where small quantities are 

taken over time. Although each instance may appear insignificant, the cumulative effect 

results in substantial financial losses. Additionally, there is a lack of real-time detection, 

as conventional systems rely on periodic inventory checks or audits, leading to delayed 

identification of theft. 

Traditional security systems such as CCTV cameras, alarm systems, and access control 

mechanisms are primarily designed to detect major breaches or unauthorized entry. 

However, they are ineffective in identifying subtle changes such as minor weight 

reduction or partial removal of goods. Furthermore, insider threats pose a significant 

risk, as individuals with authorized access can exploit system limitations without 

raising suspicion. 

Another major issue is inventory mismatch and financial loss, where discrepancies arise 

between recorded and actual stock levels, affecting supply chain efficiency, order 

fulfillment, and financial accounting. Moreover, the lack of automated monitoring 

systems increases dependency on manual supervision, which is both time-consuming 

and prone to human error. 

To address these challenges, there is a clear need for an advanced system that can 

continuously monitor inventory in real time, detect even minor changes in stored goods, 

and provide instant alerts. The system should operate autonomously and integrate 

multiple detection mechanisms to improve accuracy and reliability. 

The proposed Partial Theft Detection System using Sensors and Alert Modules fulfills 

these requirements by combining sensor technology with IoT-based communication. 

This approach ensures real-time monitoring, accurate detection, and immediate 

notification, making it an effective and efficient solution for modern inventory security 

systems.

RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

occurs gradually, and lacks real-time monitoring. This created the need for an 

automated detection system.

Sensors were connected to the microcontroller, load cell was configured, alert modules

triggers alerts, and sends notifications.

The system was tested for accuracy, threshold settings, and noise reduction. Calibration 

ensured proper weight measurement and reliable detection.

effective detection of partial theft with minimal errors. 

 

 
 



SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE  

6.1 Overview of System Architecture 

The Partial Theft Detection System is based on a modular design that integrates sensing, 

processing, alert, and communication components. The microcontroller acts as the 

central unit, collecting data from sensors, analyzing it, and triggering alerts when any 

abnormal activity is detected. 

 

6.2 Block Diagram Explanation 

The system is divided into four main layers: 

1. Sensing Layer 

This layer collects real-time data using sensors such as load cell, IR sensor, ultrasonic 

sensor, reed switch, and vibration sensor to detect weight changes, object presence, and 

tampering. 

 

2. Processing Layer 

The microcontroller (Arduino/NodeMCU) processes sensor data, converts signals, 

applies threshold logic, and identifies abnormal conditions. 

 

3. Alert Layer 

This layer provides immediate alerts using buzzer (sound), LED (visual), and LCD 

(display) to notify nearby users. 

 

4. Communication Layer 

GSM and WiFi modules are used to send SMS alerts and cloud notifications for remote 

monitoring. 

 

6.3 Key Features of Architecture 

The system offers real-time monitoring, multi-sensor accuracy, modular and scalable 

design, and reliable communication through both local and remote alert systems. 

 

 
 



HARDWARE COMPONENTS  

7.1 Microcontroller (Arduino Uno / NodeMCU) 

The microcontroller is the central unit that controls the entire system. It reads sensor 

data, processes it using programmed logic, and activates alerts or communication 

modules when abnormal conditions are detected. 

 

7.2 Weight Sensor (Load Cell + HX711) 

The load cell measures the weight of stored items, while the HX711 converts the signal 

into digital form. The system compares current weight with a baseline value and triggers 

an alert if a significant change is detected. 

 

7.3 IR Sensor 

The IR sensor detects the presence or absence of objects by using infrared light. It helps 

in identifying item removal and supports theft detection. 

 

7.4 Ultrasonic Sensor 

This sensor measures distance using sound waves. It is used to detect changes in object 

position or identify if items have been removed. 

 

7.5 Magnetic Reed Switch 

The reed switch detects whether a lid or container is opened or closed. It triggers an 

alert immediately when unauthorized access is detected. 

 

7.6 Vibration Sensor 

This sensor detects physical movement or disturbances. It helps identify tampering or 

forced handling of the storage unit. 

 

 

 

 

 
 



7.7 Alert Modules 

The system uses buzzer, LED, and LCD for local alerts. The buzzer provides sound 

alerts, LED indicates system status, and LCD displays information such as weight and 

alert messages. 

 

7.8 Communication Modules 

The GSM module sends SMS alerts, while the WiFi module enables cloud connectivity 

and remote monitoring through IoT platforms. 

 

7.9 Power Supply 

The system is powered using a battery or adapter with a voltage regulator to ensure 

stable and continuous operation. 

       

 

 

 

 

 
 



SOFTWARE COMPONENTS  

8.1 Introduction 

The software controls all system operations, processes sensor data, and triggers alerts. 

It is developed using Embedded C in the Arduino IDE, providing a simple and efficient 

platform for microcontroller programming. 

 

8.2 Development Environment 

The Arduino IDE is used for programming, offering easy code uploading, serial 

monitoring, and library support for sensors and communication modules like HX711, 

GSM, and WiFi. 

 

8.3 Programming Language 

Embedded C is used to implement system logic, handle input/output operations, and 

ensure fast and efficient execution. 

 

8.4 Software Modules 

 Sensor Reading Module: Collects data from load cell, IR, ultrasonic, reed switch, and 

vibration sensors.  

 Data Processing Module: Processes sensor data, applies calibration, and filters noise.  

 Decision-Making Module: Uses logic to detect conditions like weight drop, lid 

opening, or tampering.  

 Alert Control Module: Activates buzzer, LED, and LCD when theft is detected.  

 Communication Module: Sends SMS alerts via GSM and data to cloud via WiFi.  

 

8.5 Sensor Calibration 

Calibration ensures accuracy by setting baseline weight, defining thresholds, and 

removing noise from sensor readings. 

 

 

 

 
 



WORKING PRINCIPLE 

9.1 System Initialization 

When powered ON, the microcontroller initializes all sensors and records the baseline 

weight for comparison. 

 

9.2 Continuous Monitoring 

The system continuously monitors weight, lid status, object presence, and vibrations 

using different sensors. 

 

9.3 Data Processing 

Sensor data is processed by the microcontroller, compared with baseline values, and 

evaluated using threshold logic. 

 

9.4 Detection Conditions 

The system detects theft if there is a significant weight drop, lid opening, object 

removal, or unusual vibration. 

 

9.5 Decision Making 

If any abnormal condition is detected, the system confirms theft. Detection becomes 

more reliable when multiple sensors trigger. 

 

9.6 Alert Generation 

Once theft is detected, the system activates buzzer, LED, and LCD for local alerts, and 

sends SMS or cloud notifications for remote alerts. 

 

9.7 System Reset 

After alert generation, the system returns to monitoring mode and can be reset manually 

or automatically.

CONCLUSION  

The project “Partial Theft Detection with Sensors and Alert Modules” successfully 

addresses one of the most overlooked challenges in security systems—detecting 

gradual and partial theft. Traditional systems are primarily designed to detect major 

theft incidents and often fail to identify small but continuous losses that can accumulate 

into significant damage over time. 

This project demonstrates that by integrating multiple sensors such as load cells, IR 

sensors, reed switches, and vibration sensors, it is possible to create a robust and 

intelligent detection system. The concept of multi-sensor fusion enhances accuracy and 

ensures that no suspicious activity goes unnoticed. 

The use of a microcontroller enables real-time processing of sensor data and immediate 

decision-making. Additionally, the inclusion of both local alert systems (buzzer, LED, 

LCD) and remote communication (GSM and WiFi) ensures that users are notified 

instantly, regardless of their location. 

One of the major strengths of this system is its cost-effectiveness and scalability. It can 

be implemented in small storage units as well as expanded to large warehouse 

environments with minimal modifications. 

Overall, this project provides a practical, efficient, and modern solution for improving 

inventory security. It highlights the importance of IoT-based systems in transforming 

traditional security approaches into smart and proactive monitoring solutions
